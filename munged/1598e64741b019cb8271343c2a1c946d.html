Vicegerent

From Wikipedia, the free encyclopedia

 This article does not cite any references or sources. (November 2007)

Vicegerent is the official administrative deputy of a ruler or head of state. (Latin: vice (in place of) + gerens (governing).)

The title vicegerent is a synonym for Vice-regent.[citation needed]

[edit] Notable Vicegerents

  • Thomas Cromwell, Vicegerent of Henry VIII, appointed 1535
  • The Byzantine Emperors held as a title "God's Vicegerent on Earth".
  • The fourth Caliph in Islamic history Imam ALI ibn-e- Abutalib is believed to be Vicegerent of Allah according to Shia Muslims.
